PAROTTA RECIPE


DESCRIPTION OF THE RECIPE

The parotta is an Indian flatbread which is usually served on a banana leaf traditionally. It is one of the most tastiest and liked foods in the world, even though it's not that healthy.

The above image represents how would this flatbread look after preperation, which is a huge process even though the ingredients involved is less. This recipe will guide you through the preperation steps.

INGREDIENTS

  • All Purpose flour (3-4 cups)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Sugar (1 tbsp)
  • Oil (consistently throughout)
  • Semolina (2 tbsp)
  • Ghee (2 tbsp)

STEPS

  1. firstly, in a large mixing bowl take 3 cup maida, 2 tbsp rava, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp salt and 2 tbsp ghee.
  2. rumble and mix well until the flour turns moist. Now add water and start to knead the dough. Add water slowly and knead to a smooth and soft dough.
  3. further add 2 tbsp oil, cover and rest for 1 hour. After 1 hour, punch and knead the dough again.Knead until the dough absorbs all the oil. Now pinch a ball sized dough and place in a bowl.
  4. Add ¼ cup oil, cover and soak for 1 hour. Now take the ball and roll gently. Pull and spread as thinly as possible. Cut into thin strips using a sharp knife. Bring together the stips and pull slightly. Now roll spiral, making sure all the strips are intact.
  5. with greased hand, pat and spread the dough. Roll slightly thick, making sure the layers are intact. Now place the rolled parotta onto hot tawa. Make sure to grease the tawa well. Cook on medium flame until the base is cooked.
  6. flip over and spread ½ tsp oil. Cook on medium flame until both sides turn golden brown. Crush the parotta gently, this helps to separate the layers.
  7. Finally, enjoy your parotta!
